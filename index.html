Executive Summary & Tooling Matrix

Understand the commercial importance of ScaleStick maintenance and prepare materials.

The **SS-10 ScaleStick™** is the primary defense for high-value downstream assets in commercial water filtration systems. By dispensing a specifically formulated food-safe compound, it inhibits hard mineral scale (limescale) formation, protecting internal boilers and water lines in critical equipment like espresso machines and ice makers. Adherence to this protocol ensures maximum operational uptime and extends the lifecycle of facility technical assets.

Interactive Procedure Guide & Simulator

Follow the steps and execute the virtual maintenance sequence to learn the mechanics.

Phase 1: System Shut Down & Depressurization

Isolate the water supply and neutralize internal pressure before breaching the housing to prevent forceful discharge and seal failure.

1.1
Cartridge Preparation
Remove replacement cartridge packaging. Peel off all clear plastic shrink wrap and paper instructional bands completely. (Rationale: Obstructions prevent compound dispensation).
1.2
Water Supply Isolation
Locate the main water shut-off valve on the top left of the manifold. Rotate the red handle perpendicular to the pipe to the OFF position. (Rationale: Prevents continuous leaking/flooding during service).
1.3
Pressure Relief & Catch Bin Setup
Place the large catch bin under the manifold assembly. Fully open the blue-handled relief/flush valve on the bottom. Leave this valve OPEN to drain all built-up pressure and built-up water. (Rationale: Vents trapped energy to safely break the housing seal).

Phase 2: Removal of Depleted Unit

Manage residual water held within the clear plastic housing during the mechanical removal process.

2.1
Reposition Catch Bin
Move the catch bin directly beneath the clear plastic housing on the far right of the manifold.
2.2
Unscrew Clear Housing
Grasp the housing with both hands and rotate counter-clockwise (twist to the left). Maintain a firm grip as a large volume of water discharges into the bin. (Rationale: Be prepared for water spill; keep catch bin aligned).
2.3
Empty & Inspect
Dump the depleted ScaleStick cartridge and remaining water into the bin. Check the interior of the clear housing for sediment or debris. Wipe it clean if necessary.

Phase 3: Installation & Housing Reattachment

Sanitary handling and precise cartridge orientation are required to maintain food-safe ratings and mechanical integrity.

3.1
Documentation & Dating
Dry the exterior of the clear housing. Apply a blank label and write the current service date and your technician initials with the marker. (Rationale: Essential for maintenance log compliance).
3.2 (CRITICAL)
Orientation Check & Cartridge Alignment
• The flat bottom of the ScaleStick must rest securely over the raised circular guide tab inside the housing.
• The end with the black rubber O-ring (O-band) MUST face UP toward the ceiling (manifold bracket).
3.3
Housing Reattachment
Balance the new ScaleStick upright in the housing. Lift it to the manifold bracket, align the threads, and rotate clockwise (twist to the right).
3.4 (CRITICAL)
Hand-Tighten ONLY
Tighten the clear housing firmly by hand only. DO NOT use tools or wrenches. (Rationale: Over-torque will crack the plastic housing and cause immediate system failure).

Phase 4: System Recharge & Air Purging

Recharge the system gradually to prevent water hammer and ensure complete air removal from the cartridge housing.

4.1
Slow Refill Initiate
With the blue-handled relief valve still OPEN, locate the main shut-off valve and rotate the handle open VERY slowly. Do not open it all at once.
4.2
Air Purge
Allow trapped air inside the housing to sputter out through the blue relief valve as water slowly fills the clear cup.
4.3
Finalize Valve Configuration
Once air sputtering completely stops and a solid stream of water reaches the top, close the relief/flush valve. Then, fully rotate the main water feed valve to the complete open position (parallel).
