Here is the structured, step-by-step guide for performing the Cleaver-Brooks steam boiler bottom blowdown and water level safety test. Because this system operates under high temperature and steam pressure, **following these steps in exact order is critical** to avoid severe steam burns, flooding, or catastrophically damaging the equipment.

### **System & Unit Overview**

This commercial low-pressure steam boiler system supplies domestic hot water and laundry heat loops. Over time, evaporated water leaves behind suspended solids and minerals that settle as sludge at the bottom of the boiler drum. A regular **bottom blowdown** is required to eject this sludge under pressure and verify that the low-water safety cut-off switches are operational, extending the life of the boiler and maintaining safety standards.

### **Required Tools**

* High-temperature, heat-resistant safety gloves (minimum 250°F rated)
* Face shield and safety glasses
* Boiler logbook and pen
* Flashlight

---

## Replacement Procedure

1. **Verify Water Level:** Phase 1: Pre-Blowdown.
Locate the vertical gauge glass on the side of the boiler column. Verify the water level is sitting at exactly the half-way mark. *Warning: Do not proceed if the water level cannot be visually confirmed.*


2. **Confirm Burner State:** Phase 1: Pre-Blowdown.
Look at the control panel indicator lights and listen to verify that the boiler burner is currently firing or is ready to cycle. The blowdown test must trip the active burner to verify safety switch relays.


3. **Flush Water Column:** Phase 2: Safety Check.
Locate the column drain valve at the bottom of the gauge glass. Open this valve fully for 5 seconds to flush out mud and sediment from the column chamber, then close it securely.


4. **Test Low-Water Cut-Off:** Phase 2: Safety Check.
Locate the drain valve below the primary Low-Water Cut-Off (LWCO) chamber. Open the valve fully to drain the chamber. Watch the burner; it **must immediately shut off (trip)**. Once the burner trips, close the valve. Verify the boiler automatic water feeder recovers the level and resets the burner.


5. **Isolate Blowdown Valves:** Phase 3: Bottom Drum Blowdown.
Locate the bottom drain lines under the boiler drum. Identify the two valves connected in series: Valve A (Quick-Opening lever valve closer to the boiler) and Valve B (Slow-Opening wheel valve further from the boiler). Put on your heat-resistant gloves and face shield.


6. **Open Quick-Opening Valve:** Phase 3: Bottom Drum Blowdown.
Pull the lever handle of Valve A (Quick-Opening) to the fully open position. *Note: No water or steam will discharge yet because the downline Valve B is still closed. This prevents erosion on Valve A.*


7. **Discharge Sediment (CRITICAL):** Phase 3: Bottom Drum Blowdown.
Firmly grip the wheel of Valve B (Slow-Opening). Slowly turn the wheel counter-clockwise until it is fully open. You will hear rushing steam and sludge. Let the line flush for exactly **5 seconds**, then slowly turn the wheel clockwise to close Valve B completely. *Warning: Never slam Valve B shut, or water hammer will fracture the pipe welds.*


8. **Secure Quick-Opening Valve:** Phase 3: Bottom Drum Blowdown.
Push the lever of Valve A (Quick-Opening) back to the fully closed position to seal the isolation line.


9. **Verify and Document Recovery:** Phase 4: Recharge.
Watch the gauge glass to confirm the automatic water feeder fills the boiler back to its normal operating level. Inspect both Valve A and Valve B for any steam leaks or drips, then document the date, time, and safety status in your official boiler logbook.
